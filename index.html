Know Your Rights. Run Your Property.

LandlordRules is a free, plain-English guide to landlord and tenant rights in all 50 U.S. states. Look up the law for your state, generate notice letters, download free lease and rental form templates, and check local rent prices — all in one place, with the actual statute behind every answer.

Landlord–Tenant Laws for All 50 States

Every state sets its own rules for security deposits, the notice a landlord must give before entering, how much notice is required to end a tenancy, and limits on late fees. Our state law pages summarize these rules clearly and link directly to the official statute so you can verify the details yourself. Whether you are a landlord setting up a lease or a tenant trying to understand your rights, start with your state.

Free Legal Templates & Documents

Download free, ready-to-use rental documents as editable Word files or print-ready PDFs. The library includes a residential lease agreement, rental application, move-in and move-out inspection checklist, security deposit forms, lead-based paint disclosure, pet addendum, rent increase notice, and more. Tenants can grab letters too — a notice to vacate, a repair request, a security deposit return request, a roommate agreement, and a lease-break request.

Local Rent Price Data

Search any state, city, or zip code to see typical median and average rent, a breakdown by bedroom count, a multi-year rent trend with a forecast, and local population and resident-turnover figures sourced from the U.S. Census Bureau. Landlords use it to price units to the market; tenants use it to check whether their rent is fair.

Answers for Tenants

Can my landlord enter without notice? Keep my deposit? Raise the rent mid-lease? Our tenant Q&A gives a clear yes, no, or it-depends answer to the most common questions, each backed by the relevant law, plus practical tips for renting smart and protecting your deposit.
